Background

On 30-03-2020 the preprint Forecasting COVID-19 impact on hospital bed-days, ICU-days, ventilator-days and deaths by US state in the next 4 months was posted to medRxiv by the Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) and Christopher Murray [1].

Since that time they have been updating their results with new data at their main web site here, though the underlying assumptions and approach have not changed as of this writing.

On 31-03-2020 the Associated Press (here via the New York Times) reported that White House coronavirus task force adviser Dr.Deborah Birx singled out projections by the [IHME] at the University of Washington in Seattle, suggesting that model is close to how government experts see the situation. [2]

What the model is

At its core, a Bayesian GLMM to model log cumulative death rates at different locations (states, cities, nursing homes, etc). By rate they mean per million people; it can be thought of as a per capita cumulative death count. More details:

  • link function is the sigmoid error function, which has a more extreme slope than a logistic curve (and they say fit the data better)

\[ D(t ; \alpha, \beta, p)=\frac{p}{2}\left(\Psi(\alpha(t-\beta))=\frac{p}{2}\left(1+\frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}} \int_{0}^{\alpha(t-\beta)} \exp \left(-\tau^{2}\right) d \tau\right)\right. \]

  • outcome \(D(t ; \alpha, \beta, p)\) is the log cumulative death rate at time \(t\)
  • \(\alpha\) defines the growth rate / speed of infection
  • \(\beta\) is the time of the maximum growth rate, at the inflection point
  • \(p\) defines the maximum level the log death rate can reach

The model is fitted by specifying a model for the parameters \(\alpha, \beta\), and \(p\), and adding a normal error term to the function \(D\) above. All are indexed by location \(j\). So we have

THE FOLLOWING IS TAKEN ALMOST DIRECTLY FROM THEIR DOCUMENTS:

  • \(S_j\): social distancing covariate value at location \(j\)

  • \(y_j^t\): cumulative death rate in location \(j\) at time \(t\)

  • Measurement model:

\[ \begin{aligned} \log(y_j^t) &= \frac{p_j}{2}\left(1+ \frac{2}{\sqrt{\pi}}\int_{0}^{\alpha_j(t-\beta_j)} \exp\left(-\tau^2\right)d\tau\right) + \epsilon_{t,j} \\ \epsilon_{t,j} & \sim N(0, V_t) \end{aligned} \]

  • \(\beta\)-model specification, without or with covariate: \[ \begin{aligned} \beta_j &= \beta + \epsilon_j^\beta\text{ or }\beta + \gamma_j S_j + \epsilon_j^\beta \\ \gamma_j &\sim N(\overline \gamma, V_\gamma) \text{ (if included in model)}\\ \epsilon_j^\beta &\sim N(0, V_\beta) \end{aligned} \]
  • \(\alpha\)-model specification: \[ \begin{aligned} \alpha_j &= \exp(\alpha + u_j^\alpha) \\ u_{\alpha, j} & \sim N(0, V_\alpha) \end{aligned} \]
  • \(p\)-model specification, without or with covariate: \[ \begin{aligned} p_j & = \exp(p + u_j^p) \text{ or } \exp(p + \gamma_jS_j + u_j^p)\\ \gamma_j &\sim N(\overline \gamma, V_\gamma) \text{ (if included in model)}\\ u_{p,j} & \sim N(0, V_p) \end{aligned} \]

In this example, the user specifies

  • prior mean \(\overline \gamma\)
  • variance parameters \(V_t, V_\gamma, V_\beta, V_\alpha, V_p\).

They estimate:

  • fixed effects \(\alpha, \beta, p\)
  • random effects \(\{\gamma_j, u_j^\alpha, u_j^\beta, u_j^p\}\)

Exponential link functions are used to model non-negative parameters \(\alpha, p\).

JOSHS INTERPRETATION & FURTHER DETAILS:

Each location \(j\) can given a different center/inflection point, \(\beta+\gamma_jS_j\), which is assumed to vary depending on the covariate (whether the location is following social distancing). Notably, the inflection point was constrained to be between \(t = 20\) and \(t=80\) for all locations.

The speed parameter \(\alpha\) is not affected by the covariate, but may vary by state.

The maximum \(p\) also varies by state, and may vary by the social distancing covariate \(p+\gamma_jS_j\) if it is included in the model.

The prior mean \(\overline{\gamma}\) was estimated from Wuhan data. Short-range models were allowed a much higher variance (10x!) \(V_{\gamma}\) than long-range forecasts.

They tested four models: Two short-range models, one including \(\gamma_jS_j\) for \(\beta\) and one including it for \(p\), and two long-range models, again with one including \(\gamma_jS_j\) for \(\beta\) and one including it for \(p\).

Ensembles of the four models were then interpolated to create the final posterior draws.

Forecasting hospital use

From the paper:

Data on licensed bed and ICU capacity and average annual utilization by state were obtained from the American Hospital Association. We estimated ICU utilization rates by multiplying total bed utilization rates by the ratio of ICU bed utilization rates over total bed utilization rates from a published study. Observed COVID-19 utilization data were obtained for Italy21 and the United States, providing information on inpatient and ICU use. Data from China30 were used to approximate inpatient and ICU use by assuming that severe patients were hospitalized and critical patients required an ICU stay. Other parameters were sourced from the scientific literature and an analysis of available patient data.

Standardized death rates by population age structure.

Key assumptions to highlight

  • Assumes social distancing will be taking place across all jurisdictions until May.
